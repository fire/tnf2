What is TNF2?


TNF2 is based loosely on the original game TNF (The New Frontiers) http://www.geocities.com/TimesSquare/Castle/2979/ written and designed by Jerry Revell Jerry@starchase.demon.co.uk.


Simply put the game is about space colonization. Each player runs a corporation that is in the business of making money. You can make money in many ways including collecting resources from distant planets or moons and selling them on earth, designing new robots and leasing your design to other to build, or even piracy.


All of your activities are done away from earth by robots and ships. You create programs that control them and tell them what to do. You then upload your program and initialize it. Your robot or ship will then run, sending back the logs you requested. If all goes well you robot will finish its mission and return with valuable resources. This may take several different programs to do and depending on the activities it may take a long time.

This turns on and off facilities. All facilities are ENABLED by default. This mean they will try to consume any required supplies or resources to activate themselves. If there are sufficient consumables the facility will become ACTIVE, otherwise the facility will be INACTIVE. If the faciltiy is DISABLED it will be listed as such. By ENABLING and DISABLING facilities you can choose which facilities will use your available supplies. You may also wish to sell the surplus supplies to other adjacent facilities. See the SETFEE for selling and MAXFEE for buying.

Facilities in the same locaiton can share supplies that are provided. This means a solar power plant can power a mine if they are both at the same locaiton. By using CONNECTOR SUPPLY technology provided supplies can be shared with facilities in adjacent location. Adjacent locations are locations north (not inculding poles), south (not inculding poles), east, west, up (but not to orbit) and down (but not to surface).

Facilities are only activated at the beginning of the turn. This means if you move a stockpile of petroleum into your combustion power plant during the turn, it will not be listed as active on your next turn report. It will become ACTIVE on the following turn when the petroleum is available at the beginning of the turn. The facilitity will change from inactive to active at the beginning of the next turn when the petroleum is first available. This means that even though the facility is listed as inactive it will become active and usable the turn before you see it as active on your turn report.

The exception to this is research facilities. Research facilities are only used by Corporate commands (see RESEARCH and CONTRCT). For this reason only facilities that WERE activive the previous turn will be available for research the next turn. This means that even if you DISABLE a research facility or fail to supply sufficent consumables, if it was listed an ACTIVE on your turn report it can preform research on the next turn. While the following turn it will not be listed as ACTIVE and therefor nolonger researchable. This allows for corporations to CONTRACT at your facilities without having to deal with failure due to facilities shutting down. CONTRACTS require some degree of stability.

What about programming?

Programs are define between a PROGRAM line and an end line. These programs can then be uploaded to a robot or ship. Programs are only defined for the turn they are created in unless they are PATENTED (see corporate command PATENT). A PROGRAM - END BLOCK has the following form.


PROGRAM <name> <description>

<robot program lines here>

END


Programs name must be only one word and is case sensitive. The description may be any number of words.


Blank lines in your program will be ignored. Blank lines after a label will be changed into a NOOP commands.


PROGRAM space test

LABELA:


LOG looping

GOTO LABELA

END


You can add comments at the end of a line by using the ";". Here is an example.


LOG the time is $_time;Show me the time


The last line should either a return or an exit.


The description is optional and will only be used when you can register programs and lease them to other users. They will see the name and description and lease price. Then if they want they can UPLOAD their program and your SUBROUTINE and then GOSUB into your subroutine anywhere in their program.


PROGRAM test

END


PROGRAM Test Program Name test program description

END


Programs are stored only during the turn they are defined. If you wish to save a program for more than one turn you must PATENT it. Be sure you use good names and descriptions when PATENTING.



What about Programming Commands?


Programs is TNF2 use a macro language to get things done. That means you don't have to worry about firing thrusters for 120 ms and correcting gyro drift, activating arm servo 11A and 13B, or other complicated problems. You just give your robot high level commands.


ATTACK <id>

Attempts to attack identified Robot, Ship or Facility. Combined Weapon and Armor ratings are compared. If the armor rating is more than twice the weapon rating no damage can be done. Each attacked facility, robot or ship will get one counter attack. Damage will be at least 1% and up to the total weapon rating minus the total armor rating.

So if my robot has Heavy Work Arms and Laser, my combined weapon rating is 5 (2+3). My opponent has Open Hull and Box Hull with a total rating of 5 (1+4). So my attack damage rate is at 1 minimum since 5-5=0. If I attack a robot with no armor my attack damage rate is at 5. Attacking get a 50% bonus to damage so in this case I my final attack damage rate 1, 7.5. Counter attacking gets no bonus so it remains at 5. Damage applied is between 1% and the damage rate (IE 1%-7.5%)

Counter attack is not possible without endurance or operational status for facilities.

What about Endurance?

Endurance is your robot's power and your ships fuel. Each type of ship uses power differently. Robots use power every day. They run on batteries that need to be recharged over time. Robots can be recharged by other robots or ships and recharge themselves. Robot endurance is in months of operation. Robots that wish to recharge other robots do so by sharing their power. You can only share your power if you have more than the robot you wish to share with. If my robot has 9 months endurance and I recharge a robot with 1 month of endurance, both robots end up with 5 months endurance ((9+1)/2 = 5).

Ships power is based on generating it from fuel. Ships endurance is only changed when they move, since electrical power uses such a very small amount of fuel. Ship endurance is calculated in months, but each month equals the amount of fuel to lift off or reenter earths gravity. Smaller Planets and moons use less power based on their gravity. (IE the moon's gravity is .16 G so it uses .16 month fuel to reentry or lift off). Ships are not designed for effectient movement on planet surfaces so they use one 2 days fuel (.06) to move from surface location to surface location. Moving in and out of facilities doesn't use fuel. Ships are even worse at subterranean movement which cost 4 days fuel (.12). Ships and robots can refuel ships and ships can refuel themselves. Refueling is done by consuming rocket fuel stockpiles. Stockpiles are consumed first from inside the Ship doing the refuel and then from stockpiles at the same location.


When you fuel runs out your Ship or Robot will HALT. That mean it will not execute any more program lines until it is recharged or refueled. Once it is powered up it will immediately continue with the program if it is still running.

Materials


These include RAW or Natural materials that you can COLLECT from planet surfaces, and also include REFINED materials that are more useful materials having their impurities removed, and also include produced materials that require multiple refined or raw materials to build. These produced materials are the most valuable and are used to build robots and prefabs.


Refining


Consumes raw materials and produces equal number of refined material


Producing


Consumes raw or refined materials and produces equals mass of refined material. Some production requires more than one raw or refined material, in that case you must produce in amounts so that the mass used will equals the mass created.


Constructing


Building a facility from a prefab. The facility is given a unique ID after completion. Once prefab is constructed its owner is registered by the completing robot. This registry is permanent and can only be changed by auctioning the facility off or other official means of transfer.


Salvaging


If its broken, don't fix it just salvage it. You collect valuable Spare Parts. from junked facilities and units. But you can only salvage them once they are completely destroyed. I'm sure you can find a way to speed them along. Robot can salvage on their own with the proper technology. A facility can give any robot the ability to salvage faster even with out its own ability. By combining the two your robot can salvage at incredible speed.


Repairing


When you see your things wearing down, you can have the repaired. You can repair robots, ships or facilities. Facilities report each turn on there status, while robot and ships must be programmed to log their damage. (i.e. LOG $_damage). Repairs use Spare Parts. based on the mass of the object being repaired. A 100 ton facility that is 20% damaged will require 20 tons of spare parts to repair full. Repairing with 10 tons will result in a (10/20=50%) 50% reduction in damage (to 10% or less). Robot can repair on their own with the proper technology. A facility can give any robot the ability to repair faster even with out its own ability. By combining the two your robot can salvage at incredible speed. Robots cannot repair themselves.
